# Macro Stress Dashboard for Daily Local Monitoring
**Purpose:** Build a locally hosted dashboard app that updates daily and monitors the integrated macro stress chain:

**marine war-risk / shipping disruption -> oil supply shock -> higher energy import bills -> global dollar funding stress -> weaker foreign marginal UST demand -> repo stress / basis trade unwind -> Treasury market dysfunction -> Fed backstop of repo, UST market, and offshore dollar plumbing -> higher medium-term inflation / repression risk**

This spec is written for Codex to implement a practical desktop/local web app.

---

## 1. Product Goal

Create a **locally hosted dashboard** that helps a user monitor whether the global macro system is moving from:
1. **Sticky inflation**
2. **Convex inflation / funding stress**
3. **Break / repression**

The app should refresh data **daily by default**, with optional manual refresh and optional intraday refresh for market data.

The dashboard should answer:

- Is the Hormuz / marine insurance shock becoming persistent?
- Is physical oil scarcity spreading into freight and broader inflation inputs?
- Are large energy importers scrambling for dollars?
- Is foreign marginal demand for U.S. Treasuries weakening?
- Is repo stress / basis trade stress building?
- Is the Fed being forced to backstop both the Treasury market and offshore dollar funding?
- Is the system shifting from ordinary inflation into a nonlinear repression regime?

---

## 2. Core Conceptual Chain To Encode

The app should explicitly model this causal chain as a visible panel / flow diagram:

1. **Marine war-risk cancellation / tanker disruption**
2. **Oil and LNG supply shock**
3. **Brent backwardation / freight spike**
4. **Higher energy import bills for major U.S. creditors**
5. **Global scramble for USD liquidity**
6. **Reduced foreign willingness to add UST duration**
7. **Dealer balance-sheet saturation / repo stress**
8. **Hedge fund Treasury basis trade unwind**
9. **UST auction weakness / Treasury volatility**
10. **Fed support via SRF / repo / swap lines / FIMA / QE-style purchases**
11. **Inflation persistence, repression risk, and asset-price regime shift**

This chain should not be decorative. It should drive the app’s regime scoring.

## 6. The 10 Most Important Daily / Near-Daily Indicators

These should be the headline indicators and drive alerts.

1. **Brent M1-M3 or M1-M2 spread**
   - signal of prompt physical scarcity

2. **VLCC / dirty tanker freight rate proxy**
   - signal of physical shipping disruption

3. **EUR/USD and JPY/USD cross-currency basis**
   - signal of offshore dollar funding stress

4. **SOFR minus effective Fed funds / target midpoint**
   - signal of repo funding stress

5. **MOVE index**
   - signal of Treasury volatility

6. **UST auction tails and bid-to-cover**
   - signal of weak duration demand

7. **FIMA repo facility usage**
   - signal that foreign official holders need dollars against Treasuries

8. **Fed swap line usage**
   - signal of eurodollar/offshore dollar stress

9. **Consumer credit deterioration**
   - card delinquencies, auto delinquencies, bank charge-offs

10. **Private credit markdown / gate stress index**
   - manually maintained if fully automated feeds are unavailable

---

## 7. The 5 Intraday / Fast-Moving Stress Signals

These should be grouped into a dedicated “Crisis Monitor” panel.

1. **Treasury market depth / liquidity proxy**
2. **SOFR vs Fed funds / target**
3. **Cash-futures Treasury basis proxy**
4. **FX swap-implied USD funding pressure**
5. **Brent prompt spread**

If 3 or more trigger simultaneously, show a **systemic stress alert**.

## 8. Regime Logic

Build a rule-based regime engine first. Keep it simple and transparent.

### Regime 1: Sticky
Conditions:
- Brent prompt spread elevated but not extreme
- cross-currency basis mildly negative
- repo functioning normally
- UST auctions acceptable
- FIMA/swap-line usage low

Interpretation:
- inflation shock visible
- plumbing intact

### Regime 2: Convex
Conditions:
- Brent backwardation widening sharply
- tanker rates elevated
- cross-currency basis materially more negative
- SOFR/repo stress building
- MOVE elevated
- weak long-end auctions
- basis trade stress visible

Interpretation:
- commodity shock is spreading into funding and duration markets

### Regime 3: Break / Repression
Conditions:
- repeated weak auctions
- persistent repo stress
- FIMA or swap-line usage elevated
- signs of Fed balance-sheet support increasing
- inflation inputs broadening
- real-return suppression likely

Interpretation:
- Fed is increasingly replacing private clearing in sovereign and dollar markets

### Suggested implementation
Create a weighted score from 0 to 100 for each regime.
Allow user-editable weights in a config file.

- Info

- Critical

Include:

## 17. Initial Indicator Threshold Suggestions

These should be configurable.

### Oil / shipping
- Brent M1-M2 backwardation > 2 = warning
- > 4 = critical

### Dollar funding
- JPY basis < -30 bps = warning
- < -50 bps = critical

### Repo
- SOFR > target midpoint + 25 bps = warning
- + 50 bps = critical

### Volatility
- MOVE > 125 = warning
- > 150 = critical

### Auctions
- 10Y or 30Y tail above recent average + 1 std dev = warning
- + 2 std dev = critical

### Fed plumbing
- FIMA usage rising 3 observations in a row = warning
- swap-line usage non-trivial and rising = critical

## 20. Final Design Principle

This app should not be a generic macro dashboard.

It should be explicitly designed to monitor this thesis:

> **A marine-insurance-driven oil shock can persist long enough to create global dollar funding stress, weaken foreign marginal demand for U.S. Treasuries, stress repo and basis trades, force Fed intervention into both Treasury and offshore dollar plumbing, and produce a nonlinear inflation / repression regime.**

Every chart, alert, and regime score should serve that purpose.
